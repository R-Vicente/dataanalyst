About Me

My path into data analysis has been anything but conventional, weaving together Anthropology (B.Sc.), Forensic Science (M.Sc.), and a focused Master's in Data Analysis and Decision Support Systems. At first glance, it might look like a zigzag — but there's a clear thread running through it all: the pursuit of patterns, the understanding of complex systems, and the power of evidence-based reasoning. Whether investigating a forensic case, decoding how contact centers operate, or building predictive models, it always comes down to one thing: data.

Along the way, I found myself increasingly drawn to the stories data can tell — not just in numbers, but in what they reveal about people and systems. Research in forensic anthropology and hands-on work in contact centers only deepened that interest, especially in understanding the human side behind the metrics.

Currently based in Coimbra, Portugal, I love diving into data using tools like Python, SQL, Power BI, SPSS, and Excel. I have a particular passion for statistical methods and Machine Learning, especially Bayesian approaches, and I enjoy uncovering hidden patterns that can inform better decisions.

I'm always excited about tackling new challenges where data can lead the way! Feel free to check out the rest of the site to learn more about what I do... or did... or at least tried!
